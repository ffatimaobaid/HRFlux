Chapter 1
Introduction
This scope document is basically about setting the purpose, background, limits, and main
outcomes for HRFlux, which is an AI-based HR automation system. The idea behind
HRFlux is to cut down on repetitive manual work and make the overall employee experi-
ence smoother. In this document, I’ll explain why the system is being created, what spe-
cific problems it aims to solve, the general technical approach, and also give an overview
of the timeline and success measures for the project. HRFlux is built around multiple spe-
cialized agents that can chat with employees, a searchable HR knowledge base, automated
handling of documents, reminders, and workflows for escalations. All these features come
together to take care of routine HR tasks and provide employees with quick, consistent,
and relevant answers. This proposal also connects back to the early research, prototypes,
and requirements that were already shared in the project presentation and slides.
1.1
Problem Statement
HR teams often spend way too much time dealing with the same small questions over
and over, like checking leave balances, providing NOCs, or explaining policies. For em-
ployees, finding clear answers is also a struggle because information is usually buried in
long documents or hidden on intranet pages. This leads to repeated questions, unneces-
sary follow-ups, and slow approvals. On top of that, HR staff end up giving inconsistent
responses, which adds to the confusion and floods their inboxes and chat channels.
The real problem is that all this manual work keeps HR people busy with routine tasks
instead of letting them focus on bigger priorities like performance management, talent
growth, or updating policies. Smaller HR teams feel this pressure even more, since even
a small rise in routine queries can eat up a huge chunk of their time. Current chatbot or
FAQ tools don’t help much either—they usually give generic, out-of-context answers that
frustrate employees and eventually push them to contact HR directly.

When it comes to generating documents, such as experience letters, NOCs, or approval
memos, most of it is still done by hand, which is both slow and error-prone. There’s
also very little automation for reminders or escalations, so deadlines and SLAs often
get missed. To make things worse, knowledge silos and poor search functions mean
employees can’t easily discover the benefits or entitlements available to them. Without
session memory, the system can’t handle multi-step or personalized interactions, which
makes it less useful. Inconsistent answers also raise compliance and audit risks, especially
when official approvals or documentation are involved.
HRFlux is being designed to tackle these problems head-on. The goal is to automate
routine HR tasks, provide accurate and timely responses, and ease the workload on HR
staff so they can concentrate on more meaningful, high-value work.
1.2
Motivation
The main drive behind this project is to make HR processes quicker, more reliable, and
more focused on employees—without having to hire extra staff. By automating routine
questions and workflows, we can cut down the time it takes to resolve issues, save costs,
and also improve how satisfied employees feel with HR support. Using a modular, mul-
tiagent setup means each agent can handle a specific area, which gives more accurate
results than a generic chatbot. A searchable knowledge base also makes sure answers are
always linked back to official policies instead of guesswork. On top of that, features like
proactive reminders and escalation workflows help the company stick to SLAs and reduce
mistakes that usually happen when everything is handled manually. Another big reason
for doing this is to actually build a working prototype, which not only shows the real ROI
of AI in HR but also helps the team gain hands-on experience in system integration, data
privacy, and managing multiple agents working together.
1.3
Project Description
We are proposing HRFlux, an AI-powered HR assistant made up of multiple special-
ized agents. Instead of one generic chatbot, each agent has its own role—for example,
LeaveBot for leave requests, PolicyBot for policy-related questions, and DocumentBot
for generating letters or memos. Since every agent handles a specific area, the answers
are more reliable and easier to track. These agents work together through a simple co-
ordination layer so they can carry out step-by-step tasks like applying for leave, routing
approvals, or creating official documents.
To answer policy questions, the system uses retrieval-augmented methods that combine

semantic search with extractive QA, ensuring that responses are grounded in the orga-
nization’s actual HR documents. A memory component also keeps track of the context
across sessions, so employees don’t have to repeat the same details every time, making
interactions feel more personal.
For documents, the system uses predefined templates (such as NOCs, experience letters,
or approval forms) to save time and reduce errors, while keeping everything consistent and
compliant. HRFlux also includes proactive reminders and notifications to prevent missed
deadlines or SLA breaches. If a case cannot be resolved automatically, it is escalated to
the right HR officer along with the full conversation history, ensuring smooth handover
and accountability.
The system has two main parts: a chatbot interface for employees and an admin dashboard
for HR staff, which lets them review, approve, or adjust outputs when needed. Built-in
safeguards like role-based access, privacy controls, and audit logs keep it compliant with
organizational standards.
To integrate smoothly into existing systems, HRFlux will connect with HRIS and payroll
platforms through APIs, allowing updates like leave status to be managed automatically.
The system will also track performance through metrics such as response time, resolution
rate, number of escalations, and hours saved for HR staff.
Development will follow an iterative approach, starting with the core knowledge base
and main agents, then gradually adding features like document automation, reminders,
memory, and admin tools. The end goal is to deliver a working prototype that reduces HR
workload, improves consistency, and demonstrates real productivity gains.
1.4
Project Objectives
• Automate answers to the top 80% of routine HR queries (leave, NOC, policy clari-
fications).
• Implement specialized agents (LeaveBot, PolicyBot, DocumentBot) with domain
accuracy.
• Build a semantic, searchable HR knowledge base that ingests policy documents and
FAQs.
• Add document automation templates for NOCs, experience letters, and approval
memos.
• Implement contextual session memory for personalized multi-step interactions.
• Provide configurable proactive reminders and escalation workflows.

Project Description
2.1
Scope
The proposed system, HRFlux, is designed in a modular way to ensure clarity, scalability,
and efficient development. Each module is responsible for a specific set of functions,
and together they achieve the overall objectives of the system. The backend modules
handle data management, knowledge processing, and AI-driven agent operations. The
agent layer is responsible for query handling, document generation, and task escalation.
On the front end, employees interact with the system through a chatbot, while HR staff
use an admin portal for management and approvals. This separation ensures that both
employees and HR teams have tailored interfaces suited to their needs. The modular
design also allows for independent development and testing, making the system more
robust. Overall, these modules work together to reduce repetitive HR tasks, improve
response times, and streamline operations across the organization.

2.2.1
Module 1: Data Collection & Database Management
This module is responsible for collecting, organizing, and storing HR-related data. It
ensures centralized access to employee records, leave balances, and policy documents,
forming the foundation of the system.
1. Employee Database – Stores employee profiles, leave balances, and payroll details.
2. Policy Document Upload – Allows HR staff to upload and manage company poli-
cies.
3. Structured HR Data – Integrates mock HRMS/Payroll data for workflows.
4. Knowledge Preparation – Prepares documents for search and retrieval.
2.2.2
Module 2: Knowledge Base & Backend Services
This module transforms stored data into a usable knowledge base and provides backend
APIs for agents. It supports semantic search, workflow management, and system integra-
tions.
1. Vector Database Integration – Embeds and indexes HR documents for semantic
search.
2. Backend APIs – Provide access to employee data, leave balances, and payroll info.
3. Workflow Engine – Handles leave requests, approvals, and rejections.
4. Search & Retrieval – Delivers accurate responses from policy documents.
2.2.3
Module 3: HR Agent Layer
This module introduces specialized AI-driven agents, each focused on a specific HR func-
tion. These agents work with backend services to resolve employee queries and generate
documents.
1. LeaveBot – Handles leave balance queries and applications.
2. PolicyBot – Responds to HR policy and guideline questions.

4. EscalationBot – Routes unresolved or sensitive queries to HR staff.
2.2.4
Module 4: Interfaces (Employee & Admin Portals)
This module provides interaction points for employees and HR staff. Employees use a
chatbot for queries, while HR staff access an admin portal for management and approvals.
1. Employee Chatbot – Allows leave applications, document requests, and policy
checks.
2. Admin Portal – Enables HR staff to manage profiles, policies, and leave approvals.
3. Query Logs – Maintains records of employee queries for monitoring.
4. Approval Dashboard – Provides tools for HR staff to handle approvals and escala-
tions.
2.3
Tools and Technologies
The development of HRFlux requires a combination of tools and technologies that cover
data storage, backend services, AI agent development, and user interface design. These
tools ensure smooth integration between modules and provide a scalable environment for
future enhancements. The major tools and technologies are as follows:
1. Programming Languages: Python for implementing AI agents, backend services,
and integration; JavaScript/TypeScript for frontend development.
2. Frameworks: FastAPI for building backend APIs and services; React.js for devel-
oping the employee chatbot interface and admin web portal.
3. Databases: MySQL or PostgreSQL for structured employee and HR data; FAISS
or Chroma for vector database to enable semantic search of policy documents.
4. AI Libraries: LangChain, AutoGen, or LangGraph for creating and managing
multi-agent workflows; Hugging Face Transformers for NLP tasks.
5. Document Processing: PyPDF2, PDFplumber, or related libraries for extracting
and embedding HR policy documents.
6. Frontend Tools: HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap/Tailwind for building responsive and
user-friendly interfaces.
